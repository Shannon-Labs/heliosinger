Industrial-design language
“Solar lighthouse”: a slim black anodised mast with a translucent white crown that glows from within when the ESP32 heartbeat LED pulses.
No visible screws—everything snaps or hides under a threaded solar-hood.
Three staggered chime tubes float 5 mm above the deck so the solenoid strikers are invisible from eye level (clean, magical).
IP54 (rain & dust) yet user-serviceable (18650 drawer slides out like a DSLR battery).

Suspension holes 22.4 % from each end (nodal points) → max sustain.
Wall thickness 1 mm gives 8-second decay at 60 dB (perfect for low-density “gong”).
Striker mass 2.3 g hardened steel ball—matches tube impedance for clean strike, no double-hit.
Electronics package (single PCB, 100 × 35 mm)
ESP32-Pico-D4, 4 MB flash, on-board PCB antenna (range 40 m LOS).
TPS63020 buck-boost → 5 V rail even when battery droops to 2.3 V (cold night).
Three 1 A low-side MOSFETs (IRLML2502) drive solenoids at 5 V, 600 mA, 50 ms pulse → 30 mJ per strike.
INA219 on solar input → logs daily harvest (OTA telemetry).
IP5306 power-path manager (same chip in Xiaomi power-banks) – seamless solar/battery switchover.
Pin header for I²C → future light-sensor or wind-speed add-on.
Gold immersion pads for pogo-test jig (factory tests 30 s per board).
Firmware cheat-sheet (already compiled)
Deep-sleep 58 s, wake, fetch NOAA JSON, map to MIDI, strike, update LED, sleep.
